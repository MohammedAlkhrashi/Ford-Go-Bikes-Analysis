Preliminary Wrangling¶

Ford GoBike is a public bicycle sharing system in the san francisco bay area, it's the first regional and large scale bicycle sharing system deployed in California. it has over 2600 biycycles in over 250 stations.

This dataset contains over 500,000+ bike trips recorded.

I will try to explore the data visually and understand relation between different features.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 543,474 Bay Wheels's bike trips recorded over the course of three months ( 2018-6-1 to 2018-8-1), there are 20 variables that desrcibe each trip and the user of the bike.

The Data:

Trip Duration (seconds)

Start/End Time and Date

Start/End Station ID

Start/End Station Name

Start/End Station Latitude

Start/End Station Longitude

Bike ID

User Type (Subscriber or Customer Subscriber = Member or Customer = Casual)

Member Year of Birth and Gender

Derived Columns:

Memeber Age

Trip Day of The Week

Starting/Ending Hour of the trip

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

Understading why duration of each trip differ seems interesting, since intuitively it seems very dependent on other variables from our dataset.

We'll will try to understand why duration differs from one trip to another by using other variables.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I suspect the age and gender of memebers will effect the duration, also I could see the day of the week effecting the duration.

The User Type(Subscriber or Customer) might effect the duration of each trip.
